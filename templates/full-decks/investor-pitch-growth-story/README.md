# Investor Pitch Growth Story

## Summary

Investor pitch and growth story archetype for moving from problem and solution to traction, market, business model, go-to-market, financial path, and ask.

## Visual traits

Pitch-style high-contrast cover, crisp traction metrics, proof-driven market pages, concise product and GTM logic, and ask-oriented close.

## Use when

Use for investor pitch decks, fundraising stories, board fundraising updates, growth narratives, and company/product pitch materials.

## Do not use when

Do not use for diligence memos, investment committee decisions, or operating plan decks unless the audience is evaluating a fundraising narrative.

## Storyline pattern

Cover, investment answer, problem, solution, traction, market, business model, go-to-market, financial path, risk/readiness, and ask.

## Layout + component stack

Uses `metric-strip`, `outcome-support`, `ranked-bars`, `comparison-table`, `phase-roadmap`, `sensitivity-grid`, and `decision-log`.

## Theme / assets

Theme: `assets/themes/pitch.css`. Optional product or contextual image slot; no required media assets.

## Source inspiration

Source-neutral investor narrative patterns. No external deck, brand system, venture firm identity, or consulting-firm identity is copied.

## Evidence shape

Best with traction metrics, customer proof, market categories, unit economics, GTM motion, financial assumptions, risk records, and funding ask details.

## Path

`templates/full-decks/investor-pitch-growth-story/index.html`

## Related showcase patterns

`templates/showcase/pitch-traction-growth-page.html`, `templates/showcase/executive-answer-data-page.html`, `templates/showcase/business-case-sensitivity-page.html`

## Flexible swaps

Swap traction metric strip for customer proof cards when numbers are sparse, replace market ranking with comparison table when categories are qualitative, and replace financial sensitivity with milestone-based ask when forecasts are not available.

## When to diverge

Diverge when the user needs a diligence memo, acquisition recommendation, or product strategy roadmap rather than a fundraising story.

## Do not copy page order/content

Do not copy the archetype page order, placeholder metrics, footer text, or pitch labels. Use it only to understand pitch-story pacing and evidence density.
