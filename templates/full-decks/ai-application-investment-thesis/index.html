Investment answer

Investment thesis archetype
Thesis logic

A strong thesis converts fragmented evidence into a small set of investable beliefs

1Demand signal

Show the user-provided proof that the problem is urgent, recurring, and budget-backed.

2Segment focus

Separate priority wedges from broad category noise using comparable criteria.

3Winning model

Explain which business model, product shape, or go-to-market motion is advantaged.

4Defensibility

Identify the mechanism that gets stronger with usage, scale, workflow depth, or distribution.

5Risk boundary

Make the biggest uncertainty explicit and connect it to a diligence test.

6Action ask

Close with the next decision: source, diligence, invest, partner, pause, or revisit.

Evidence inventory

Use only the signals the user actually provided, then make gaps visible

3-5validated signals that support the thesis or timing.
2-4comparable segments, assets, or scenarios to prioritize.
1-3explicit risks that could reverse the recommendation.
1next decision the audience can make after the deck.
So what Data-rich prompts should become exhibits; sparse prompts should become hypotheses

Do not invent market numbers. If the user has no quantified evidence, turn this page into qualitative support and open questions.

Use chartsWhen values, categories, or comparable records are supplied.
Use tablesWhen options share common decision criteria.
Use calloutsWhen the evidence is directional or assumption-led.

Investment thesis archetype
Moat logic

Defensibility pages should show the mechanism that compounds, not only list advantages

Defensible thesisWhy the winner gets stronger after each customer, workflow, or dataset
Workflow depthThe product owns a recurring job rather than a detachable feature.
Data feedbackCorrections, outcomes, or usage improve quality over time.
Distribution edgeThe route to buyer or user is structurally advantaged.
Switching costProcesses, integrations, or compliance trails make replacement hard.
Economic pathGross margin, expansion, and implementation repeatability improve by cohort.
Platform resilienceThe value survives bundling, model price shifts, and feature imitation.
Investment thesis archetype
Diligence path

The diligence sequence should convert thesis uncertainty into specific proof requests

1Confirm demand

Test buyer urgency, budget owner, and current workaround cost.

2Validate workflow

Check whether usage sits inside the system of work or at the edge.

3Stress moat

Probe data, distribution, integration, and switching-cost claims.

4Model economics

Translate adoption, margin path, and expansion into return scenarios.

5Decide next step

Invest, partner, continue diligence, or stop with clear kill criteria.

Investment thesis archetype
Decision close

Close with the specific decision, required evidence, and fallback if proof is insufficient

NowAlign on thesis

Confirm the target wedge, decision owner, and minimum proof threshold.

NextRun focused diligence

Collect the evidence that resolves the largest uncertainty.

ThenMake capital choice

Invest, partner, continue sourcing, or stop with documented rationale.

Fallback

If evidence is too sparse for a decision, end with hypotheses and proof requests rather than a false recommendation.

The close should make the next meeting easier to decide.
