Enterprise transformation archetype
Executive answer

The transformation answer should name what changes, why it matters, and how leaders will govern it

1Decision-backwards

Start with the leadership decision, not with a catalog of initiatives.

2Few pillars

Group the change into 2-5 mutually distinct transformation pillars.

3Current-state proof

Use observed gaps, metrics, risks, or stakeholder pain to explain why now.

4Target state

Describe the new operating model, decision rights, cadence, and reusable assets.

5Governance loop

Show how progress will be measured, reviewed, escalated, and adjusted.

6Next asks

End with decisions, resourcing, constraints, and open questions.

Enterprise transformation archetype
Case for change

Use a case-for-change page when the audience must understand why incremental action is insufficient

GapCurrent performance or capability shortfall from user material.
FrictionRepeated handoff, delay, rework, cost, or quality pain.
RiskExposure that grows if the operating model does not change.
WindowTiming reason to act now rather than later.
Fallback If no metrics exist, use qualitative evidence and label the gaps.

A sparse prompt should become a clear hypothesis page, not a fake benchmark page.

Transformation logic

A transformation story needs a mechanism that explains how progress compounds

Operating
flywheel
SenseCapture demand, performance, risk, and customer or employee signals.
DecideTranslate signals into prioritized decisions and accountable owners.
ExecuteRun work through redesigned workflows, roles, and tools.
LearnPromote evidence, feedback, and reusable assets into the next wave.

When to use

Use a flywheel when each wave should reduce cost, risk, or cycle time for the next wave.

Use a process flow instead when the transformation is linear.

Workstream design

Workstreams should be grouped by the change they drive, not by organizational chart alone

1Diagnose

Baseline the current work, decision pain, and evidence gaps.

2Design

Define target workflow, roles, controls, and success measures.

3Pilot

Test with a bounded population and collect outcome evidence.

4Scale

Extend to adjacent teams and reusable capability assets.

5Govern

Review progress, risk, value, and learning in a fixed cadence.

Enterprise transformation archetype
Roadmap

A transformation roadmap should sequence proof, capability build, scale, and institutionalization

0-90 daysProve the wedge

Choose the first workflows, baseline outcomes, and define decision gates.

3-6 monthsCodify the model

Document reusable assets, ownership, controls, and adoption playbook.

6-12 monthsScale by adjacency

Extend to adjacent workflows while strengthening the shared capability base.

12+ monthsInstitutionalize

Embed metrics, governance, funding, and talent implications into management routines.

Governance cadence

Governance pages should connect cadence, evidence, decision rights, and owners

CadenceDecisionEvidence requiredOwner
WeeklyRelease or hold changesQuality, risk, adoption, and cost signalWorkstream owner
MonthlyPromote reusable assetsReuse case, documentation, and control mappingPlatform lead

Design rule

A governance page is useful only if it clarifies what leaders will decide differently.

Avoid committees without evidence, owners, or decision rights.
