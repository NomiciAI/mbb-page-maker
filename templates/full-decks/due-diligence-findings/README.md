# Due Diligence Findings

## Summary

Due diligence findings archetype for turning fact base, red flags, confirmatory tests, and implications into an invest/partner/proceed decision readout.

## Visual traits

Blue diligence style, finding-led executive answer, fact table, red-flag register, scorecard, sensitivity, and go/no-go decision close.

## Use when

Use for commercial, product, technology, operational, or investment diligence findings where the audience needs evidence, risks, and tests.

## Do not use when

Do not use for fundraising pitches, pure market scans, or M&A decision memos where the recommendation already owns the full storyline.

## Storyline pattern

Cover, diligence answer, key findings, fact base, red flags, confirmatory tests, sensitivity, recommendation implications, next diligence.

## Layout + component stack

Uses `outcome-support`, `comparison-table`, `risk-matrix-register`, `weighted-scorecard`, `sensitivity-grid`, and `decision-log`.

## Theme / assets

Theme: `assets/themes/blue.css`. No required media assets.

## Source inspiration

Source-neutral diligence and red-flag review patterns. No external deck, brand system, or consulting-firm identity is copied.

## Evidence shape

Best with findings, facts, comparable records, risk/red-flag records, confirmatory tests, scenarios, and recommendation implications.

## Path

`templates/full-decks/due-diligence-findings/index.html`

## Related showcase patterns

`templates/showcase/diligence-red-flag-page.html`, `templates/showcase/evidence-table-implication-page.html`, `templates/showcase/business-case-sensitivity-page.html`

## Flexible swaps

Swap red-flag matrix for decision log when risks are mostly actions, replace scorecard with comparison table when scores are not justified, and replace sensitivity with open-questions page when scenarios are unavailable.

## When to diverge

Diverge when the user needs a market-entry strategy, acquisition decision, or investor pitch rather than diligence findings.

## Do not copy page order/content

Do not copy the archetype page order, placeholder findings, footer text, or diligence labels. Use it only to understand findings-readout composition.
