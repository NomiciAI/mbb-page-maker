# Workshop Decision Pack

## Summary

Workshop and working-session full-deck archetype for preparing discussion, structuring exercises, capturing decisions, and assigning next actions.

## Visual traits

Neutral facilitation style, agenda pages, objective framing, option debate, decision capture, and next-step ownership.

## Use when

Use for workshops, working sessions, leadership alignment meetings, decision meetings, and facilitated planning packs.

## Do not use when

Do not use for one-way board updates, investment memos, or detailed analytics readouts unless the output is a live decision session.

## Storyline pattern

Cover, session answer, objectives, agenda, pre-read context, discussion frame, exercise/option debate, decision capture, next steps.

## Layout + component stack

Uses `objective-list`, `objective-summary`, `agenda-table`, `comparison-table`, `pros-cons-table`, `decision-log`, and `phase-roadmap`.

## Theme / assets

Theme: `assets/themes/mono.css`. No required media assets.

## Source inspiration

Source-neutral workshop facilitation and executive alignment patterns. No external deck, brand system, or consulting-firm identity is copied.

## Evidence shape

Best with session goals, topics, participants, options, decisions, owners, timing, and required pre-read facts.

## Path

`templates/full-decks/workshop-decision-pack/index.html`

## Related showcase patterns

`templates/showcase/workshop-decision-capture-page.html`, `templates/showcase/text-patterns.html`, `templates/showcase/evidence-table-implication-page.html`

## Flexible swaps

Swap timed agenda for simple agenda when no times exist, replace option debate with issue tree when the session is diagnostic, and replace decision log with open questions when the meeting is exploratory.

## When to diverge

Diverge when the user needs a pre-read deck, a post-workshop summary, or a status update rather than a facilitated decision pack.

## Do not copy page order/content

Do not copy the archetype page order, placeholder decisions, footer text, or exercise labels. Use it only to understand workshop pacing and capture mechanics.
