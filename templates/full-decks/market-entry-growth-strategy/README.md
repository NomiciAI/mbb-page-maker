# Market Entry Growth Strategy

## Summary

Market entry and growth strategy archetype for selecting where to play, how to enter, which customer segments to prioritize, and what path to scale.

## Visual traits

Green growth strategy style, market signal metrics, segment and competitor comparison, entry option matrix, risk framing, and phased entry roadmap.

## Use when

Use for market entry, growth strategy, regional expansion, segment prioritization, channel strategy, and category expansion decks.

## Do not use when

Do not use for investor pitches, pure market scans, or pricing strategy decks unless the main decision is where and how to enter.

## Storyline pattern

Cover, growth answer, market attractiveness, customer/segment opportunity, competitive position, entry options, recommendation, risk and roadmap.

## Layout + component stack

Uses `metric-strip`, `ranked-bars`, `comparison-table`, `portfolio-prioritization-matrix`, `risk-matrix-register`, and `phase-roadmap`.

## Theme / assets

Theme: `assets/themes/green.css`. Optional generated cover asset: `assets/media/covers/market-growth-cover.jpg`. Interior visual support can use `visual-market-map` when the message is about opportunity spaces before quantified scoring.

## Source inspiration

Source-neutral market entry and growth strategy patterns. No external deck, brand system, or consulting-firm identity is copied.

## Evidence shape

Best with market size/growth, customer segments, competitor or channel records, entry options, right-to-win evidence, risks, and timeline.

## Flexible swaps

Swap ranked bars for market-size table when exact values matter, replace entry matrix with weighted scorecard when criteria are explicit, and replace roadmap with journey map when customer adoption is central.

## When to diverge

Diverge when the user needs a market scan only, an investor pitch, or a commercial/pricing strategy rather than an entry decision.

## Do not copy page order/content

Do not copy the archetype page order, placeholder market labels, footer text, or option names. Use it only to understand market-entry pacing.
