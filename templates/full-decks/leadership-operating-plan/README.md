# Leadership Operating Plan

## Summary

LOP, annual operating plan, and quarterly leadership rhythm archetype for translating strategic priorities into KPIs, initiative portfolio, budget choices, risks, and governance.

## Visual traits

Green operating cadence style, balanced KPI snapshot, portfolio prioritization, budget and capacity table, governance grid, and decision close.

## Use when

Use for leadership operating plans, annual planning, quarterly business reviews, CXO operating rhythms, budget cycles, and initiative portfolio planning.

## Do not use when

Do not use for pure progress updates, investor pitches, or narrow technical implementation plans unless operating plan approval is the main decision.

## Storyline pattern

Cover, operating answer, KPI ambition, initiative portfolio, budget and capacity choices, risk/control posture, governance cadence, decisions and next cycle.

## Layout + component stack

Uses `metric-strip`, `portfolio-prioritization-matrix`, `comparison-table`, `raci-governance-grid`, `risk-matrix-register`, and `decision-log`.

## Theme / assets

Theme: `assets/themes/green.css`. No required media assets.

## Source inspiration

Source-neutral annual planning and executive operating rhythm patterns. No external deck, brand system, or consulting-firm identity is copied.

## Evidence shape

Best with strategic priorities, KPIs, initiative records, budget or capacity constraints, risks, decision forums, cadence, and owner fields.

## Flexible swaps

Swap portfolio matrix for weighted scorecard when initiatives have explicit criteria, replace budget table with sensitivity grid when the funding decision depends on scenarios, and replace RACI grid with decision log when cadence is still undefined.

## When to diverge

Diverge when the audience needs a SteerCo status read, a budget approval memo, or a transformation story rather than an operating plan.

## Do not copy page order/content

Do not copy the archetype page order, placeholder priorities, footer text, or planning labels. Use it only to understand LOP pacing and operating-decision composition.
